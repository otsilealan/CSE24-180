About This Website

Welcome to Natural Botswana, your comprehensive guide to exploring the pristine wilderness and rich cultural heritage of Botswana. This website was created out of a passion for sharing the unique beauty and wonder of one of Africa's most remarkable destinations.

Our mission is to provide travelers with authentic, detailed information about Botswana's wildlife reserves, cultural experiences, and practical travel advice. Whether you're planning your first safari or are a seasoned explorer of southern Africa, we aim to enhance your journey with insider knowledge and inspiring content.

Having spent over a decade exploring Botswana's diverse ecosystems, from the sprawling waterways of the Okavango Delta to the vast expanses of the Kalahari Desert, we hope to share our love for this extraordinary country and help you create your own unforgettable experiences.

About Botswana

Key Facts about Botswana

Geography

Landlocked country in Southern Africa, dominated by the Kalahari Desert and Okavango Delta, covering an area of 581,730 square kilometers.

Climate

Semi-arid with hot summers (October-April) and dry, mild winters (May-September). Best wildlife viewing during the dry season when animals gather around water sources.

Population

Approximately 2.4 million people, with most concentrated in the eastern parts of the country. Batswana (singular: Motswana) is the term for citizens of Botswana.

History

Formerly the British protectorate of Bechuanaland, Botswana gained independence in 1966 and has maintained a stable democracy ever since, making it one of Africa's success stories.

Culture

Rich in traditions with major ethnic groups including Tswana, Kalanga, and San (Bushmen). Known for traditional crafts, music, and storytelling.

Economy

One of Africa's most economically and politically stable countries, with diamond mining, tourism, and cattle farming as major industries.

Botswana Travel Essentials

Currency

Botswana Pula (BWP). ATMs available in major towns, but carry cash for remote areas. Major hotels accept credit cards.

Language

English (official) and Setswana (national). English is widely spoken in tourist areas and major establishments.

Visa Requirements

Many Western countries receive visa-free entry for up to 90 days. Check current requirements before travel.

Health Information

Malaria precautions needed in northern regions. Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from endemic countries.

Transportation

4x4 vehicles recommended for self-driving. Charter flights common for reaching remote safari camps.

Safety Tips

Generally safe destination. Wildlife precautions essential. Follow guide instructions during safaris.
